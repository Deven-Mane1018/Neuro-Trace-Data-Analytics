42 PD patients · 5,875 voice recordings · 6 months longitudinal · 22 acoustic features · 2 UPDRS targets
JITTER FAMILY Cycle-to-cycle frequency variation · laryngeal tremor
Jit% JitA JitR JitP JitD
Clinical meaning: Micro-fluctuations in vocal fold vibration frequency. Cycle-to-cycle deviations reflect involuntary laryngeal tremor — a hallmark of basal ganglia dysfunction. Jitter(%) consistently ranks #1 across all three feature importance methods.
SHIMMER FAMILY Amplitude instability · neuromuscular control degradation
Shim% ShimB SAQ3 SAQ5 SA11 SDDA
Clinical meaning: Instability in the amplitude of vocal fold oscillation. Reflects neuromuscular control degradation affecting breath support and phonation. Shimmer(%) shows novel fast-progressor elevation — an original NeuroTrace finding.
HARMONIC Breathiness and turbulent airflow ratio
NHR HNR
Clinical meaning: NHR and HNR quantify the ratio of harmonics to noise in the voice signal. HNR declines monotonically with PD severity, achieving Cohen's d = 0.89 — a large effect. Replicates Sakar et al. 2013.
NONLINEAR Complex vocal dynamics · nonlinear signal entropy
RPDE DFA PPE
Clinical meaning: Nonlinear dynamics and entropy measures capture what jitter/shimmer miss. PPE escalates across all 3 disease stages (KW H=312.4, p<0.001). Replicates Tsanas et al. 2010. PC1 explains 41.2% of total variance.

7-Layer Research Pipeline

Each layer builds on the last. Each decision is methodologically justified.

01

Advanced Data Engineering

KNN imputation · IQR winsorisation [1%, 99%] · RobustScaler · disease staging

Before transforming anything, we audit. KNN imputation preserves inter-feature correlations. Winsorisation at 1–99th percentile keeps sample size intact — critical in clinical datasets where removing outliers removes real pathology. RobustScaler handles the heavy tails typical of bioacoustic data.

02

Longitudinal Trajectory Analysis

Per-patient OLS regression · slope phenotyping · centroid curves

Every patient has a unique disease story. We fitted 42 individual OLS regressions to classify Fast (β₁ ≥ p66), Moderate, and Slow Progressors. Fast progressors accumulated 24.3 UPDRS units over 6 months vs 4.1 for slow — a 5.9× difference invisible in aggregate statistics. Most projects never look at the patient level.

03

Multi-Dimensional Correlation

Pearson r · Spearman ρ · Kendall τ · partial correlation (age/sex controlled)

Three correlation methods computed simultaneously. Partial correlation controls for age and sex — because jitter correlates with age independently of PD. Uncontrolled correlations overstate biomarker utility and introduce confounding. Spearman ρ = 0.83 for Jitter(%) after Bonferroni correction replaces the naive r = 0.89.

04

Hypothesis Testing Battery

Shapiro-Wilk · Mann-Whitney U · Kruskal-Wallis · Bonferroni · Cohen's d

Normality rejected for all 16 biomarkers (Shapiro-Wilk, p < 0.001). That single test decision cascades through the entire analysis — non-parametric tests throughout. Bonferroni corrects for multiple comparisons. Cohen's d quantifies practical significance, not just statistical. 5 formal hypotheses. 5 supported.

05

Dimensionality Reduction Suite

PCA biplot · scree plot · t-SNE cluster maps

PC1 captures 41.2% of variance. The scree plot identifies a clear elbow at component 3. t-SNE at perplexity=30 produces three well-separated stage clusters in 2D — confirming the biomarker space encodes clinically meaningful disease stage information. The structure is in the data. You just need the right lens to see it.

Predictive Biomarker Ranking

The Early Detection Index

An original composite metric. Not in any published paper. Built here.

What it is

A logistic regression classifier trained on the top 5 permutation-importance biomarkers, normalised to [0,1]. The predicted probability of belonging to early-stage PD (UPDRS < 20) becomes a continuous clinical screening score.

Why it matters

Individual biomarkers give thresholds. The EDI gives a probability. It integrates complementary signal from jitter, shimmer, HNR, and nonlinear dynamics — the full vocal spectrum of Parkinson's — into a single actionable number.

What it means clinically

EDI > 0.70 Early stage profile. Clinical follow-up recommended.
EDI 0.40–0.70 Moderate dysphonia. Reassessment in 3 months.
EDI < 0.40 Significant degradation. Advanced progression risk.

Cross-validated AUC = 0.78 ± 0.04 across 5 folds
